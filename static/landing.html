The Problem

It doesn't have to be.

How the AI Works

Spatial reasoning, not guesswork

Our AI doesn't claim to see through piles. Here's what it actually does.

📏

Reference Library

60+ common junk removal items with real L×W×H dimensions. The AI recognizes these items in your photos and uses their known size as measuring references.

📐

Spatial Calibration

The AI uses known-size objects as rulers — a couch at 84×36×34 inches, a door at 80×32 inches, even electrical outlets and wall studs at 16" spacing. More reference points visible = better calibration.

⚙️

Your Review, Your Call

Every estimate shows which items were detected. You can uncheck items that stay, adjust the scope, and the price recalculates instantly. The AI assists — you decide.

About WhatShouldICharge

WhatShouldICharge was built by Kevin Hayford, a junk removal operator with over 10 years of field experience in Oshkosh, Wisconsin. After thousands of jobs — and too many underpriced estimates — he built the tool he wished he'd had starting out: an AI that measures volume from photos so operators can quote with confidence from day one.

WhatShouldICharge is an AI-powered estimation tool built specifically for junk removal businesses. Operators upload customer photos and receive cubic yard volume calculations, itemized breakdowns, and price ranges in under 30 seconds — without visiting the job site.

The AI uses spatial reasoning with a reference library of 60+ common junk removal items (furniture, appliances, electronics, construction debris) with real-world dimensions. It identifies items in photos, measures them against known references, and calculates total volume in cubic yards. Price ranges are based on each operator's own $/CY rates for standard and heavy loads.

Pricing is pay-per-use with credit packs — no monthly subscriptions, no contracts, no cancellation fees. A single estimate costs $5. Credit packs reduce the cost to as low as $1.50 per estimate (250-Pack for $375). Credits never expire. New accounts include 5 free estimates with no credit card required.

WhatShouldICharge also offers an embeddable customer widget that junk removal companies can add to their websites. Customers upload photos and submit contact info, and the operator receives the AI estimate along with the lead — each submission uses one credit.

The platform is expanding beyond junk removal to serve dumpster rental, cleanout services, moving, and other volume-based industries.
